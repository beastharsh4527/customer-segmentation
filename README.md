# Customer Segmentation (K-Means Clustering)

Segmenting mall customers into distinct groups using k-means clustering on the Mall Customers dataset, based on annual income and spending score.

## What I did
- Loaded and explored customer data (age, income, spending score)
- Applied k-means clustering to group customers by income and spending behaviour
- Used the elbow method to confirm the optimal number of clusters (5)
- Visualised and interpreted the resulting customer segments

## The 5 segments found
- High income, high spending: the VIP customers to retain and reward
- High income, low spending: the biggest untapped opportunity
- Average income, average spending: the reliable core customer base
- Low income, high spending: enthusiastic spenders, loyal but higher risk
- Low income, low spending: budget-conscious, lower priority for premium offers

## Skills shown
- Unsupervised machine learning (k-means clustering)
- Choosing the right number of clusters with the elbow method
- Data visualisation with matplotlib
- Translating clusters into actionable business segments

## Takeaway
The standout finding is the high-income, low-spending group: customers who have money but aren't spending it. Spotting them is the first step to targeting them and growing revenue.
